Preserving the places
that made us who we are

Latvia's history is written not only in its archives — but in the farmsteads, townhouses, and manors where generations were born, raised, and buried. Through deportations, wars, and the great diaspora of the 20th century, millions of Latvians were severed from the land their families had worked for centuries.

Saknes — meaning roots in Latvian — is a living map built on the 1935 topographic survey of Latvia. Anyone can mark the property their family once called home, attach names and faces to those addresses, and find connections they didn't know existed.

Whether your family left Latvia in 1905, 1944, or 1990 — there is a place for them here.

🏚
The 1935 Map

We use the pre-war Latvian topographic survey — the most accurate record of the country before the occupations. Streets, farms, and buildings that no longer exist are visible exactly as they were.

👨‍👩‍👧
Community Built

Every pin on the map is added by a person with a connection to that place. Saknes is a living record, grown by the diaspora, for the diaspora.

🌍
Global Reach

Latvian communities exist across Australia, Sweden, the USA, the UK, Canada, and beyond. Saknes gives them a shared place to gather around the land they came from.

📜
In Both Languages

All content can be submitted in Latvian or English. We are building for the generation that remembers, and the generation that is rediscovering.

"Kas tēva zemi mīl, tas māju zūdot raud — whoever loves their father's land weeps when that home is lost."
— Latvian proverb
I
Remember

The first act of resistance against forgetting is to name. Saknes exists so that every family property — however modest — can be placed back on the map of Latvia.

II
Connect

Families who lived on the same street in 1930 may now have grandchildren scattered across three continents. Saknes is where those threads can meet again.

III
Preserve

Oral history dies with its last speaker. By putting names, photographs, and stories onto the map, we create a record that will outlast any individual memory.

Your family's place is waiting

It takes five minutes to add a property. Your contribution connects Latvians everywhere to the land they came from.
